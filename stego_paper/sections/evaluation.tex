\section{Evaluation}
\label{sec:evaluation}

This section provides a comprehensive assessment of the proposed workflow, specifically focusing on its embedding capacity, perceptual imperceptibility, and operational robustness.

\subsection{Capacity Analysis}
The channel capacity of the semantic layer is fundamentally dependent on the dimensionality of the generated \textit{TangentsDB}. We analyzed the variance in dictionary size across heterogeneous subreddit contexts, observing that the number of extracted tangents, $N$, directly dictates the bits-per-message throughput, defined by $\log_2 N$. Our results indicate that complex, multifaceted discussions yield significantly higher embedding capacities compared to narrow or highly restricted thematic environments.

\subsection{Perceptual \& Statistical Imperceptibility}
Imperceptibility is evaluated through the dual lenses of semantic coherence and contextual integration. Unlike traditional steganographic methodologies that risk producing disjointed or statistically anomalous text, our intent-based approach ensures that every stego-contribution maintains a clear thematic alignment with the encompassing discourse. Comparative analysis using the MAUVE metric \cite{pillutla2021mauve} and cross-entropy (perplexity) assessments demonstrates that our generative framework preserves high levels of naturalness. This is particularly significant given that standard perplexity scores may not fully capture the nuances of long-context semantic quality \cite{wang2022perplexity}.

\subsection{System Robustness \& Recovery Rate}
The deployment of a recursive verification mechanism represents a critical component in ensuring high payload fidelity. Our experimental data reveals that this mechanism successfully identifies and remediates ambiguous or off-topic outputs in approximately 70\% of initial generation failures. This recursive refinement significantly enhances the bit recovery rate, ensuring consistent payload integrity even when utilizing non-deterministic LLM sampling for cover text synthesis.

\subsection{Resistance to Automated Steganalysis}
The system's resilience against automated detection is primarily derived from its high-level semantic alignment. When subjected to standard linguistic steganalysis benchmarks \cite{Wang_2023}, the generated comments proved to be indistinguishable from legitimate human interactions within the same conversational thread. This suggests that shifting the embedding domain to communicative intents effectively bypasses the detection signatures typically exploited by token-level steganalysis.
