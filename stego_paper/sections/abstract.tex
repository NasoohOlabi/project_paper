We present a novel, context-aware \textbf{black-box} steganographic framework that leverages Large Language Models (LLMs) to embed secret payloads within high-level semantic communicative intents. Unlike traditional methodologies that manipulate token distributions or token-selection heuristics, our approach achieves imperceptibility by integrating steganographic content into the pragmatic flow of threaded social media discussions. The system employs a \textbf{two-layer embedding architecture}: the primary layer encodes information via the strategic selection of a conversational reply target, while the secondary layer utilizes a dynamic repository of contextual tangents (thematic variants of a related response) derived from context-aware search and retrieval. To ensure operational synchronization between sender and receiver without direct metadata exchange, we implement a deterministic search algorithm and a synchronized LLM configuration. Efficiency is further enhanced by a custom linguistic compression layer. A closed-loop verification mechanism guarantees that the hidden intent is robustly recoverable prior to transmission. Experimental results demonstrate that our framework effectively addresses the tension between statistical security and contextual naturalness, producing stego-text that is statistically indistinguishable from human interactions and highly resistant to advanced semantic steganalysis.
