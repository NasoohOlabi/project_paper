\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This research introduces a novel semantic steganography framework that redefines the embedding domain by leveraging the advanced generative capabilities of Large Language Models. By transitioning from traditional token-level manipulation to the encoding of secret payloads into high-level communicative intents, we effectively address the pervasive tension between statistical security and contextual naturalness. The proposed system ensures that steganographic contributions are not merely linguistically fluent but are pragmatically integrated into the target discourse, rendering them highly resistant to both human observation and automated semantic analysis.

The implementation of a closed-loop verification mechanism significantly enhances system robustness, guaranteeing payload recoverability prior to public transmission. Our findings demonstrate that the proposed multi-layered semantic embedding approach provides a highly effective and secure methodology for covert communication in dynamic social media environments. Future work will explore the scalability of this approach across diverse platforms and the potential for multi-agent competitive environments to further refine the imperceptibility of generated covers.
